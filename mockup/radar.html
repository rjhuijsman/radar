A living radar scope for your 4-inch round display.

Centered on home, it sweeps live ADS-B traffic, airports and your own points of interest — and pins the flights you care about, even when they're off the edge of the map.

Verdict

Yes — every piece is a well-trodden path on this hardware.

The 5793 panel and 5800 Qualia board are a designed-to-pair combo, and each capability you asked for maps to an established ESP32-S3 technique. Here's the honest breakdown.

Display & compute Fits

720×720 lives in PSRAM

A 16-bit framebuffer is 720×720×2 ≈ 1.01 MB; double-buffered ≈ 2 MB, comfortable inside the Qualia's 8 MB octal PSRAM.

Driven with Arduino_GFX (or LVGL) over the ST7701S RGB-666 dot-clock bus. A radar moves slowly, so sub-second refreshes look smooth.

Wi-Fi onboarding Standard

Hotspot when it's lost

No network on boot → the S3 raises a SoftAP Radar-Setup captive portal to collect credentials, exactly like the reference project.

Once joined, it serves the config browser via ESPAsyncWebServer; settings and POIs persist to flash (NVS / LittleFS).

Live traffic API-fed

ADS-B without a receiver

Poll a bounding box around home from a public feed — airplanes.live or adsb.fi (point+radius JSON), or OpenSky as fallback.

Each state carries position, altitude, ground speed, track and callsign — everything the symbology needs.

Special flights Recommend iCal

TripIt, the pragmatic way

TripIt's API is OAuth 1.0a — painful on-device. Instead poll your private TripIt .ics feed and parse the flight segments.

Match segment flight numbers to live callsigns to promote a blip to amber; manual add covers anything TripIt misses.

POIs & airports Rendered

Icons, and drawn runways

POIs are small JSON records added in the browser and drawn as cyan glyphs with labels.

Airports store runway heading(s) so the scope can draw the actual strips — entered by hand or fetched from OurAirports when you add one.

Off-map targets Pure render

The edge arrow

A special flight beyond range never disappears: it clamps to the rim as an amber chevron at its true bearing, with a live distance readout.

Same treatment scales to several tracked flights at once without cluttering the center.

Controls · try it

One encoder, two toggles — turn it yourself.

Drag the knob, press it, work the switches. Turn browses targets and, while following, a list of flights that includes Home; hold + turn zooms; a press commits — follow the selected flight (the map slides over to it), switch to a browsed flight, or drop back home. Pressing at home with nothing selected steps between saved homes. The 3-way Display switch picks flights / both / weather; Geography overlays borders + cities; Sleep powers the panel down with a CRT-style collapse — and waking is a clean restart.

Before you order parts

Open decisions & honest caveats.

None are blockers — but each is a call worth making up front.

01
Feed choice & limits

OpenSky now wants OAuth2 client credentials for its free daily allowance; airplanes.live / adsb.fi offer simpler point-radius JSON. Poll every 5–15 s and cache.

02
Callsign matching

TripIt gives a flight number (BA117); ADS-B broadcasts an ICAO callsign (BAW117). Promoting a blip to "special" needs an airline IATA→ICAO map.

03
Weather reprojection

RainViewer serves Web-Mercator PNG tiles; the scope is azimuthal around home. Resample per pixel — cheap at ≤100 NM, and rain only refreshes every ~5 min.

04
Basemap storage

Natural Earth coastlines/borders/cities are bundled as packed polylines. A Europe subset fits flash; the whole continent at detail wants a microSD on the broken-out SPI header.

05
Sleep = clean restart

The Sleep toggle deep-sleeps the S3 on an RTC-wake pin; nothing is kept in RAM, so waking runs a fresh boot. Blank the backlight (via the PCA9554) before sleeping, since I²C is off while asleep.

06
Secrets at rest

Wi-Fi password and the private TripIt URL sit in flash. Fine for a home device; worth noting before sharing one.
